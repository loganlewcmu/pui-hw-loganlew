    • Take the ferry to St John

    OVERVIEW

    St. John is the least populous island in the USVI, and home to many stunning beaches and hikes. Enjoy a relaxing ferry ride to St. John from the nearby Red Hook port.

    My family’s favorite beaches are Cinnamon Bay (white sands, tranquil water) and Trunk Bay (great for snorkeling).

    It’s also an excellent place for a sunset dinner. Just be sure to catch your ferry home!

    Visit Magens Bay and eat eat eat

    OVERVIEW

    Magens Bay is St. Thomas’ most-loved beach, and for good reason. Whether you need a calm morning swim, an afternoon tan, or an evening swim accompanied by a beautiful sunset view, Magens always delivers.

    If you want to visit Magens but get off the beaten path, hike the Magens Bay Beach Trail

    When you’re done, grab a bite at one of our favorite local restaurants.

    Go on a chartered boat tour

    OVERVIEW

    St. Thomas sits in one of the prettiest seas I have ever seen, and there are many ways to get out and enjoy the water. Book an excursion, ranging from turtle-spotting to a sunset dinner cruise.

    Don't forget to pack motion sickness medication if needed. And bring some extra cash to tip your boat crew!

    Eat fresh at a local farmers market

    OVERVIEW

    Market Square, located downtown, is open Saturdays from 5am - 4pm

    Bordeaux Farmer's market is located out west and open on the 2nd and last Saturday every month from 10am-2pm.

    Twice per month, local farmers convene in the island’s westernmost region to sell their homegrown foods. Enjoy food, music, and the island’s agrarian life.

    Visit Water Island

    OVERVIEW

    Located across the bay from Charlotte Amalie is Water Island. Quaint and with stunning views, it makes for a perfect afternoon to evening trip.

    Take the Water Island Ferry from Crown Bay Marina, snorkel at Supermarket Reef, and visit Fort Segarra (an abandoned WWII outpost). End the evening with a dinner at one of the island’s two restaurants, and enjoy the stunning view of Charlotte Amalie on the boatride home.

    Visit the historic Frenchtown

    OVERVIEW

    St. Thomas was colonized by various European forces in the 17th and 18th centuries. French colonists became the islands’ predominant fishermen, and they settled in what is now called Frenchtown (my parents’ and grandparents’ birthplace).

    Follow in my childhood footsteps and watch the fisherman bring in their daily catch (if you arrive early in the morning). You can then learn more about the island’s history at the French Heritage Museum and poke your head into a local restaurant or bar.
